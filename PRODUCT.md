# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Store managers, inventory clerks, and cashiers running pet shops. They use this system during business hours, at a counter or back-office desk, under time pressure — receiving invoices, checking stock, processing sales. They care about getting tasks done fast with zero confusion; they are not here to appreciate the interface.

## Product Purpose

Unified retail platform for pet shops: ERP admin (purchasing, inventory, products, customers, orders), PDV (point-of-sale), and e-commerce storefront — all powered by a single NestJS API. The admin is where operators manage the business day-to-day. Success means an operator can receive a supplier invoice, link all items to catalog products, confirm stock entry, and get back to the floor in under three minutes.

## Brand Personality

Modern, clean, efficient. Warm earthy palette (forest green, orange, sage, cream) anchors brand identity without feeling cozy or artisanal. The interface is a professional tool with a distinctive visual voice, not a generic SaaS dashboard.

## Anti-references

- **SAP / Oracle ERP**: dense, overcrowded screens, nested menus five levels deep, no visual hierarchy, feels like legacy enterprise software from 2005. Never. Every screen must breathe.
- **Notion / Linear**: excessive minimalism, editorial typography, feels like a notes app. This is operational software; it must communicate status, action, and urgency clearly — not retreat into whitespace and subtle grays.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness.** Every element on screen must earn its place by serving an operator task. Decorative complexity is a cost, not a feature.
2. **Urgency surfaces.** Pending actions (unlinked items, draft invoices, low stock) must be visually impossible to miss. Operators shouldn't need to search for what needs attention.
3. **Speed first.** The most common action on any screen must be reachable in one interaction. No buried menus for daily workflows.
4. **Consistent density.** Not spartan like a notes app, not dense like a legacy ERP. Tables have breathing room; data-rich screens use space deliberately.
5. **Brand without noise.** The warm palette is the identity; motion and visual accents amplify action rather than decorate space.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Good practices (readable contrast, keyboard navigation, visible focus states) applied as development standards. No formal WCAG audit planned for the current phase.
